Learning Difficulties

Note: the term “learning disability” means different things in different parts of the world (see below).

Dyslexia

Dyslexia is a learning difficulty characterised by problems in phonological processing and trouble with reading and spelling in spite of normal intelligence. In the USA and Canada, dyslexia is categorised as a “learning disability”. However, in the UK, learning disability refers to a reduced intellectual ability, so dyslexia is referred to as a specific learning disability. In Australia, the term “learning difficulty” is also used in this context. The fifth edition of the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM 5) uses the term “specific learning disorder”; see What Is Specific Learning Disorder? on the website of the American Psychiatric Association (APA), who publishes DSM 5.
